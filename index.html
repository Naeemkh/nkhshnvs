I obtained my BSc--Engineering and an MSc--Earthquake Engineering in 2009 and 2012, respectively. The focus of my master's thesis at Iran University of Science and Technology dealt with probabilistic seismic risk assessment for the City of Tehran, Iran. In August 2013, I joined the Center for Earthquake Research and Information (CERI), where our group has conducted a good amount of externally funded research. Additionally, we successfully landed other research proposals, and I have participated in most of them. My work has included the validation of synthetic seismograms for the 2008 Chino Hills and La Habra earthquakes in the Los Angeles Basin, the latter of which is part of a cooperative research project. I have investigated new ideas and helped develop methods that have improved analysis and made processing more efficient. As an example, I developed two data processing packages and applied a machine learning approach that optimizes the attenuation parameters used in the simulation. My research focuses on development of synthetic seismic data in a regional and local (including site response analysis) scale and optimizing seismicity parameters that are validated with recorded data. Interdisciplinary studies are inevitably part of modern research and provide keys for knowledge discovery. I am working intensely on applying machine learning and natural language processing methods in Earth Sciences, Seismic Risk Mitigation, and other related fields.
